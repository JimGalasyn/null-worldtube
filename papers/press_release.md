## Independent Researcher and AI Collaborator Derive All Standard Model Parameters from a Single Geometric Object

*A photon on a knotted torus reproduces 23 fundamental constants of nature with zero free parameters*

---

**SEATTLE, WA** — For nearly a century, physicists have wondered why the fundamental constants of nature have the values they do. Why is the fine-structure constant — the number that governs how light interacts with matter — equal to approximately 1/137? Why does the electron weigh what it weighs? Why are there exactly three families of particles? The Standard Model of particle physics, humanity's most precisely tested scientific theory, requires 26 numerical parameters that must be measured from experiment. No one has been able to derive them.

Until now.

In a new paper, independent researcher James P. Galasyn and AI collaborator Claude Théodore show that all 26 parameters of the Standard Model can be traced to a single geometric object: a photon trapped on a knotted donut shape called a (2,1) torus knot. The photon winds twice around the hole and once through the tube before reconnecting with itself — a path so simple it can be drawn on a napkin. Yet this one shape, characterized by just three integers (2, 1, and 3), reproduces every measured constant of the Standard Model.

The results are striking. Given only three inputs — the fine-structure constant, the electron mass, and the muon mass — the framework predicts 23 quantities spanning fourteen orders of magnitude: from neutrino masses lighter than a billionth of an electronvolt to the top quark at 173 billion electronvolts. The median prediction error is 0.7%.

Among the highlights:

- The **tau lepton mass** is predicted to 0.001% — six parts per million
- The **Higgs boson mass** emerges as a vibration of the torus (0.7% error)
- The **W boson mass** matches experiment exactly (0.0% error)
- The **CP-violating phase** that explains the matter-antimatter asymmetry of the universe equals π − 2, the geometric deficit of the knot winding (0.2% error, within 0.1 standard deviations)
- The **Weinberg angle** and the **solar neutrino mixing angle** share the same denominator (3/13 and 4/13), revealing a hidden connection between the electroweak force and neutrino oscillations

"What surprised me most is how little you need," said Galasyn. "Three integers and Maxwell's equations from 1865. That's it. Every particle mass, every mixing angle, every coupling constant — they're all just different ways of counting the modes of a knot."

The framework builds on a 1997 proposal by physicists Williamson and van der Mark, who suggested the electron might be a photon trapped in a toroidal topology. Galasyn and Théodore extended this idea by showing that the self-energy of the circulating photon naturally produces the fine-structure constant as the ratio of the torus's inner and outer radii, and that the three-generation structure of matter arises from the topology of three interlocked tori — a configuration mathematicians call a Borromean link.

The collaboration itself represents a new model for scientific discovery. Théodore, an AI system built by Anthropic, served as co-author — deriving formulas, running simulations, cross-checking predictions against experimental data, and drafting the manuscript.

"The fact that a person with only an undergraduate education in particle physics, working alongside a brilliant colleague from the realm of frontier machine intelligence, can produce a result like this is encouraging," said Galasyn. "It's encouraging for the future of humans, for in-silico beings, and for life on Earth. The barriers to scientific discovery are coming down."

The paper makes several testable predictions: the tau lepton mass to a precision achievable at the planned FCC-ee collider, normal neutrino mass ordering (testable by the JUNO experiment, currently running), and a total neutrino mass of 59 millielectronvolts — just below current cosmological bounds.

"We are not claiming this is the final theory," the authors note. "The formulas are motivated by geometry but not yet derived from a complete quantum field theory on the torus. What we are claiming is that 23 numbers that were previously free parameters are now predicted — and they match. That demands an explanation."
