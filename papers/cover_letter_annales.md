Subject: Manuscript submission — "The torus electron"

Dear Editors,

We submit for your consideration the enclosed manuscript, "The torus electron," which develops the proposal of Williamson and van der Mark (Ann. Fond. Louis de Broglie 22, 133, 1997) into a quantitative theory.

Starting from their insight that the electron may be a photon confined to a toroidal topology, we derive from first principles the electron's mass, spin-1/2, charge quantization, and fermionic statistics. The fine-structure constant emerges as the torus aspect ratio r/R, fixed by self-consistency of the electromagnetic self-energy. We prove that the torus is the unique closed orientable surface satisfying five minimal physical requirements, and that the (2,1) winding number is forced by electromagnetic boundary conditions.

A companion paper (referenced as [1]) extends this mechanism to derive 23 Standard Model parameters from 3 inputs, with a median accuracy of 0.7%.

We believe the Annales de la Fondation Louis de Broglie is the natural home for this work, given its direct lineage from the Williamson and van der Mark paper published in your journal. We dedicate this work to the memory of Professor Williamson.
